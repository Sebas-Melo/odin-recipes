Carbonara

Description: Carbonara is the simplest yet one of the most delicious dishes Italy as brought to life. This icon of the italian cousine consists on a pasta base (usually, spaghetti or penne), a creamy sauce made with egg yolks, parmiggiano cheese and pecorino cheese, decorated with crispy dices of guanciale.

Ingredients

  • 400g of spaghetti or large diameter penne
  • 200g of guanciale
  • 4 egg yolks
  • 70g parmiggiano reggiano
  • 40g pecorino romano
  • black pepper

Steps

  1. Fill a large pot with lightly salted water and bring to a rolling boil. Stir in pasta and return to a boil. Cook pasta uncovered, stirring occasionally, until tender yet firm to the bite, about 12 minutes. Reserve a little pasta water, then drain.
  2. Meanwhile, heat a skillet over medium heat; add diced guanciale, cook and stir until crisp, 5 to 10 minutes.
  3. Whisk the egg yolks in a bowl together with the grated cheeses and ground black pepper to taste. Add pasta water until mixture is creamy.
  4. Stir in the crispy guanciale, add the pasta and the egg and cheese cream, and toss until evenly coated.
